Self Introduction:

My name is Min Park

Hi, my name is Min Park and as of 2025 I am currently a sophomore attending University of Wisconsin-Madison. Since 2015, one of my prominent hobbies has been speedcubing. Speedcubing refers to a practice of solving rubik’s cube as quick as possible; you may have seen from youtube videos of some people solving 3x3 cubes in as fast as 3 seconds, and in fact, the current WR of 3.08 seconds have been set this very month (February 2025). Speedcubing is widely recognized as a respectable hobby sport, and official competitions are held at regular intervals throughout the world. While I’m nowhere near countless other better speedcubers than me out there, I still do consider myself as an adept one. For reference, my current personal best is 8 seconds, and I normally average around 12 seconds. Now, if you happen to be no speedcuber - and ever had sought solving the standard 3x3 Rubik’s cube by yourself for the first time, you are in the right place. While there are numerous methods for solving 3x3 cubes out there, the current most popular method is the CFOP method developed around 1981(which is also the one I use). In this website, I plan to walk you through the beginner version of the CFOP method in terms of learning, and efficiency. While the actual CFOP method is naturally far more complex, learning this beginner’s version should easily pave you the way to advancing towards the real version, and above all, give you the satisfaction of solving your first cube. The tutorials will mainly consist of an image showing the ultimate goal of a specific step in the method, and a following video explaining how. I’m certainly not the most eloquent tutor, but I hope I will have delivered enough information and intuition for you to understand in one go. In a nutshell, speedcubing is no magic - it is really nothing but a train of thought involving memorizations of algorithms and pattern recognition. So, if you are still at all interested in this, be an aspiring learner, and let us begin by clicking the first link that introduces you to the most fundamental step of this method : Cross.
